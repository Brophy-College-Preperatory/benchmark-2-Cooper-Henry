The Parts in Nuclear Fusion

First, a General Explination about how Nuclear Fusion is done generally.

Nuclear Fusion is the process of pushing two atoms together, which creates a larger atom.

This process releases a large amount of energy, while using very little mass.

However, the process of pushing these atoms together can be done in a variety of ways, with there being three main methods.

There are many different methods beyond just these three, but these three are the main ones. And each of these different methods require vastly different parts, actions, and tools.

However, they all do share one thing in common; the matierals.

All nuclear fusion requires the use of hydrogen and hellium, plasma, and something that creates heat, which is what creates the energy.

The Three Types of Nuclear Fusion

Gravitational Confinement

Only Possible on the Sun or other areas in the universe with high enough gravity


Uses Gravitational Forces to push atoms together, which creates the heat


The very method used to create universes, as that is pushing atoms together into one mass


Too little space of gravitational confinement to preform this method


Magnetic Confinement

Heat up a circle of plasma until it is hot enough to fuse. Has to keep a steady and confined space

Attempts to copy to what the sun does, but at a much more managable and smaller scale

Parts:

Tokamak (the large cylinder) in the center that contains magnets

Magnetic Field

Electrical Current

Plasma Current


Inertial Confinement

Compresses the plasma using lasers in nearly one billionth of a second, which makes the plasma hot enough to fuse and release energy

Much different method compared to what the Sun normally does

Parts:

Hohlraum (The piece that holds the plasma)

Inferread Laser

X-Rays

Ultraviolat Laser
